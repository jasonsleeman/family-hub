Family Hub — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 6 March 2026

Your data never leaves your iCloud. Family Hub has no backend server. We never see, collect, or have access to your family’s data.

1. Our Core Privacy Principle

Jason Sleeman (“we”, “us”, “our”) built Family Hub as a free iOS application. All information you enter — lists, meals, calendar events, chat messages — is stored exclusively in your family’s private iCloud account via Apple’s CloudKit service.

If you delete the app, your data remains safely in your iCloud account. If our company ceases to exist, your data is completely unaffected.

2. What Data Is Stored

Family Hub stores the following data in your iCloud account:

  • Family information — family name, member display names, and profile avatars
  • Lists — grocery items, dinner plans, and custom lists you create
  • Calendar events — global family events and mirrored snapshots of Apple Calendar events you choose to share
  • Chat messages — your Jarvis AI conversation history and family group chat messages
  • Preferences — calendar visibility, conflict dismissal patterns, and notification settings
  • Learning data — patterns the app learns when you dismiss scheduling conflicts

All of this data is stored in your family’s private CloudKit zone. We have no access to it.

3. Apple Calendar Access

Family Hub requests access to your Apple Calendar to mirror selected calendars into the shared family view:

  • Permission-based — the app only accesses your calendars after you grant explicit permission
  • User-controlled — you choose which calendars to share; unselected calendars are never read
  • Read-only mirroring — the app reads events to display in the family calendar but does not modify your Apple Calendar
  • Local processing — calendar data is processed on your device

You can revoke calendar access at any time in Settings → Privacy & Security → Calendars.

4. Jarvis AI Assistant

Family Hub includes an optional AI assistant called Jarvis, powered by the Anthropic Claude API:

  • Bring Your Own Key — the family admin supplies their own Anthropic API key, stored in your family’s private iCloud zone, never on our servers
  • What is sent — your message, family member first names, current grocery items, tonight’s dinner plan, and upcoming calendar event titles/times
  • What is NOT sent — Apple IDs, iCloud identifiers, email addresses, or any other personally identifiable information
  • No persistent storage — each request is stateless; Anthropic does not retain your data beyond processing
  • Entirely optional — the app is fully functional without configuring an API key

For details on how Anthropic handles data, see Anthropic’s Privacy Policy.

5. Push Notifications

Family Hub uses Apple Push Notifications via CloudKit subscriptions to notify you of changes (e.g., new list items, chat messages). Notifications are delivered through Apple’s standard infrastructure and can be disabled in your device settings.

6. Widgets

Home screen and lock screen widgets display summaries of your family data. Widget data is stored locally on your device and is never transmitted to any external service.

7. Data Sharing Within Your Family

When you join a family in Family Hub, shared data is visible to all accepted members. This sharing is powered by Apple’s CloudKit sharing and controlled by the family owner. Personal list items remain private unless you explicitly share them.

9. Data Retention & Deletion

  • All data is in your iCloud account — you can delete items within the app at any time
  • Removing the app does not delete your iCloud data; to fully remove data, go to Settings → [Your Name] → iCloud → Manage Storage → Family Hub
  • We have no server, so we retain no user data whatsoever

10. Children’s Privacy

Family Hub may be used by children as part of a family group. We do not collect personal information from children independently. All data is managed within the family’s iCloud account, controlled by the family admin.
